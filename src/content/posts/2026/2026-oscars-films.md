It's Oscars time, so here's a quick rundown of my thoughts on all ten Best Picture nominees.

### My Favorites

- [One Battle After Another](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt30144839/) - My favorite film of the year. Paul Thomas Anderson at his most propulsive and crowd-pleasing, even though it's still plenty strange. Another movie feels really of the time. Benicio del Toro turns in my single favorite performance of the year.

- [Bugonia](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt12300742/) - I'm a sucker for movies that take sharp turns into sci-fi, and Bugonia delivers on that. The internet conspiracy theorist element felt very of the moment too. My second favorite film of the year.

- [Frankenstein](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt1312221/) - A take on the classic tale that had me all the way through. Jacob Elordi's performance was excellent, and it's my wife's favorite film of the year. Probably my third favorite of the year as well.

### The Rest

- [F1](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt16311594/) - Entertaining enough, even if Brad Pitt's character would have been banned from F1 racing ten times over in the course of the film.

- [Hamnet](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt14905854/) - Found most of the film a little too over the top, but the conclusion was extremely effective and moving. Jessie Buckley's performance is hard to deny.

- [Marty Supreme](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt32916440/) - A little too over the top for its own good. I was hoping for a bit of a change of pace now that the Safdies are working individually, but it felt more like Uncut Gems with ping-pong than I was expecting.

- [The Secret Agent](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt27847051/) - Didn't really come together in a way that made sense or was satisfying for me. Has a lot in common with _I'm Still Here_ from last year, which covers similar ground better.

- [Sentimental Value](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt27714581/) - Was a little too Oscar-y and distant when I first watched it, but in hindsight it has stuck with me more than I expected.

- [Sinners](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt31193180/) - Highly entertaining, but made some strange story choices that I didn't love. Can't say no to dual Michael B. Jordans though, and even though I prefer _One Battle After Another_, a late campaign surge for this film would be fun.

- [Train Dreams](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt29768334/) - Didn't love this one. Wonderfully shot, but too traditionally Oscar-y. And frankly, not enough trains. If I had to pick between the vroom vroom movie and the choo-choo movie, I pick the vroom vroom movie.
