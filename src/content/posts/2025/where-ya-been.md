I've been on a bit of a roller coaster since I last posted here.

On a cold February morning, I came in from snow-blowing my driveway only to sit down at my laptop and realize I couldn't log into Slack, and then Google. That was enough to realize I was experiencing the classic tech company goodbye. It was my first time ever being laid off, but with the current market, I couldn't be shocked. I took a few minutes to collect my thoughts, called my wife to let her know, and then it was off to LinkedIn to start the process of figuring out where to go from here.

I had an outpouring of support pretty much instantly once I posted on LinkedIn. My connections in the Drupal community really helped - without them, navigating the layoff would have been much harder. Personal connections made all the difference. If I was chasing unsolicited applications I would have lost my mind.

Community really helped me.

Contracted with Actual AI.

Explored (didn't get) some other opportunitiues, but came on full time as founding developer. Exciting things ahead.
